The knowledge, skills and commitment of teachers are the most important factors in achieving high quality educational outcomes. Therefore, it's essential to ensure that those recruited to teaching Posts are of the highest caliber and well suited to the tasks they have to fulfill.


Teacher's welfare and training programs –Timely training of teachers to learn and adapt to the changes to create a curriculum fit for the students. Skill up-gradation programs to keep them at par with the ever evolving teaching-learning process.

Co-Cirricular Activities

Co-curricular activities - They are an integral part of education. The core aim of education is to foster all round development of a child. In order to achieve this aim, there should be a striking balance between syllabus, curriculum, books and also co-curricular activities.


Academic learning and sports education complement each other and helps developing individuals with leadership quality, sharing, team spirit and tolerance.

STUDENT DEVELOPMENT AND VALUE EDUCATION

Individual attention to students - Number of students in a section, teacher student ratio, if care is taken for every child’s growth instead of focusing on select few. Counseling facilities, both for personal problems and career related issues available for students.


Value System - It aims at inculcating values to equip the learner to lead a life satisfying to the individual in accordance with the cherished values and ideals of the society.

STUDENT DEVELOPMENT AND VALUE EDUCATION

Life-skills education-Life-Skills Education helps a student to effectively cope with interpersonal problems, conflicts as well as deal with stress and frustration.


Global Outlook - Steps taken to encourage students to think globally, appreciate differing points of view and understand that the world is a complex, changing place.

PHILANTHROPIC SERVICES

Community Service-Initiatives taken to help the weaker/ disadvantage section of the society in the neighborhood.

MODE OF TEACHING AND PARENTAL INVOLVEMENT

Parental Involvement - Effective partnership between school and parents help students achieve better results.


Individualized Instruction and Approaches to Students - Different students have different needs, therefore, to effectively teach all students, different strategies should be used. The teaching and interactions with students must reflect the needs of each, with the understanding of each as individuals.There are three types of learners in class- Auditory, visual and Kinesthetic. Therefore, lesson planning should be done keeping in mind the needs of each type of learner.

INFRASTRUCTURE AND OVERALL UPKEEP

School Infrastructure - How well kept the school building is, labs, play grounds, IT enabled class rooms, computers, books in library.


Health and Hygiene - conducting talks and seminars to make the students and staff aware of various health issues that can arise from unhygienic conditions.

ACADEMIC STANDARD AND LEADERSHIP

Academic Rigor - How well the school is doing academically and its overall annual performance.


Value for Money - Is the school worth investing one’s future. Does the school provide conducive environment where holistic development takes place?

Leadership/management quality - Capability and vision of school Principal/director/CEO in improving teaching learning process, seeing to it that the staff works as a team, integrity and other leadership skills.

SOCIAL INCLUSION AND INCLUSIVE EDUCATION

Social Inclusion – Ensuring that students from economically weaker section feel as confident and secure and enjoy same facilities as students of general category.

Inclusive Education - The facilities in the school to help in the learning process of children with special needs, number of such children actually enrolled, how well are they integrated with other students.
